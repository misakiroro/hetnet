China's outbound mergers and acquisitions rebounded in the second quarter of this year with deal value increasing 148 percent quarter on quarter, according to an industrial report. Chinese investors returned to the deal table in the April-June period after a lukewarm first quarter and were the second most acquisitive cross-border nation by value with 94 deals totaling at $35.9 billion, according to international law firm Baker McKenzie's Cross-Border M&A Index.
